How to Be A Good Collaborator

More than knowledge, what I attempt to help students with is how to be a good collaborator. Of all the skills I have, the two most vital are 1) being able to collaborate effectively, and 2) project management.

1) Be willing to learn. One of the best pieces of advice that I’ve received is to not just be a calculating drone. 2) Ask questions. 3) Be willing to teach others. 4) Understand your collaborator’s motivations. 5) Be flexible. Be uncomfortable. Be vulnerable. 6) Establish ground rules for a safe collaborative environment. 7) Be professional. 8) Communicate frequently enough. 9) Listen, and then attempt to clarify. 10) Never think a project or a field is “below you”, or a “waste of your time”. Some of my best ideas have come from working in the trenches and wrangling the data. 11) Don’t work with assholes, no matter how prestigious they are. Seriously, read the [No Asshole Rule](). 12) Don’t ever assume something about the data. 13) Have fun. The best collaborators are the ones I look forward to working with. I try to maximize my time for these collaborations. 14) Value everyone’s contributions.
